Efficiency

Efficiency is a concept well rooted into the human reasoning process. Without it, every already negligent goal that has existed in the history of humanity would be far more inaccurate. Every goal is an efficiency targeted goal, and everyone who has ever argued the contrary is absolutely wrong. The issue at hand is the common definition of 'efficiency.' When used in conversation, it usually refers to production or manufacturing. But in reality, efficiency is involved in every process ever designed by any human being, no matter the goal, target, or dependent/independent variables affected. Whatever the task is, the best solution a human brain is able to find, is solving a process in more goal-oriented efficient way, and therefore satisfying a pre-existing necessity. Economics is the science dedicated to studying how to solve a problem by allocating scarce resources in a more efficient way. I made a choice to invest time studying this science, because I 'understand' the value of someone who truly understands it. The principles that dictate said person's decision making process can be applied to any industry and any task


Control

People tend to say that obsessing over control is a terrible path leading to a troubled mind, and as we live in a chaos system; influenced primarily by the initial conditions of each subset from within the system: the mind cannot comprehend, I agree. However, learning to control one's desire for control and power can make one's emotional intellect grow immensely. This gives birth to a rising current of different 'gains-of-control.' Once we 'understand' breathing as the main source of life, and as main provider of energy to our physical body, we can learn to control our emotions to such point that we gain the capacity to manipulate our perception of how the world that surrounds us affects us. This constant grasp for comprehension results in a product, a combination of will, effort, desire to learn, and patience, an imperative requirement to 'understanding.' Once it exists, it can no longer seize to do so, as once we understand something, our innate human qualities force us to develop unconscious subjective bias. Once we understand a goal in a way, we can't stop doing so, and that's when we are presented with the most important experience of them all. The one that finally determines which path our lives will take and what piece we will play in the horrifyingly beautiful bureaucratic masterpiece humanity has created to power and sustain the survival and evolution of our species: a decision. "Will we give in to the notion of powerlessness, knowing it makes us powerful, and take a strong position, to back our decisions or... will we pass them on and ceed control?"


And so, a rational consumer is either born or murdered.
